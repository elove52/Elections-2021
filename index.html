Elections 2021

A response to the need for transparency in our governance, new people for our board with new insights, new ideas, and to usher in a newer more responsive board

The Importance Of Our Elections February 12th 2021

This is your chance to bring new ideas and new members to our Board of Directors and have a direct say in how our building is governed, view candidate profiles, address key issues such as more transparency in our governance, establish a newer more invigorated sense of community with the implementation of in person events once Health Guidelines (Federal, Provincial, and Municipal) layout a framework for returning to normal socialization. New directions such as establishing a committee made up of members for our Newsletter.

Mohammadali Tofighi

My name is Mohammadali, and I am a Ph.D. in civil engineering, water resource management, and a researcher and student of disaster management at York University. I also have been property manager of a building and the manager of a construction project with more than 300 units. I am currently researching disaster and emergency management such as evacuation plans, road accidents, health management, and crisis-related to climate change. I found Neill-Wycik as a lovely place to live and offers strong support. I hope this community remains strong and lasts long, and I can help support our community.

Luc Dupuy

My name is Luc Dupuy and this is my 4th year living in the building. I am a 4th year Computer Science student at Ryerson and am planning to graduate after this semester! When I am not studying I like to do/teach archery, read, and play video games and chess with my friends! Neill-Wycik has become a home away from home for me (it's also how my parents met!) and I hope I can give back to the community and help improve the little things that I think will go a long way.

Sumera Perveen

My name is Sumera Perveen and this is my 3rd-year student living at Neill-Wycik. I am a Post-doctorate fellow at the University of Toronto, researching and developing medicines for diseases including COVID -19. In my spare time, I like to cook new recipes and share meals with my roommates. I also love traveling and meeting new people. Neill-Wycik has been my home in Canada and allowed me to be part of a great community, meeting new people from all walks of life. For that, I am incredibly grateful and I hope I can bring my dedication and passion for helping people to serve our community here at Neill-Wycik.

Ghassem Tofighi

My name is Ghassem (my nickname is Sina) and I have been in Neill-Wycik for 9 years. I completed my Ph.D. in Electrical and Computer Engineering in 2017 and now working as a part-time professor at Ryerson University and a full-time professor at Sheridan College. During the years I lived here, I held several events such as the Persian New Year events, and created the website template for our facilities, in addition to being the Makerspace facility manager, I am an advocate of freedom of speech, and I believe I can help to defend the members' rights and their best interests as a board member.
